Boucing bubbles

Ingredients Needed Warm water: 1 cup Dish soap: 1/4 cup (Dawn dish soap is recommended for best results) Sugar: 1 tablespoon
Step 1) Prepare the Mixture: Pour warm water into a small bowl. Using warm water helps dissolve the sugar more easily. Add sugar to the warm water and stir until it is completely dissolved. Gently pour in the dish soap and stir carefully to combine without creating too many bubbles.
Step 2) Let It Cool: Allow the bubble solution to reach room temperature. This helps improve the quality of the bubbles.
Step 3) Get Ready to Bounce: Put on a winter glove on one hand. The glove prevents the oils from your skin from breaking the surface tension of the bubbles, allowing them to bounce instead of pop.
Step 4) Blow Bubbles: Dip a bubble wand into the solution and gently blow bubbles. You can also create your own wand using a pipe cleaner or wire.
Step 5) Catch and Bounce: Let the bubbles land softly on your gloved hand. Gently bounce them up and down. Avoid hitting them like a ball, as this will cause them to pop.

Knedic sand

Ingredients For your convenience in creating this recipe, we’ve included shop-able ad links to some of the products used; disclosure policy available here. 2 1/2 cups colored play sand — you can also use colorful craft sand 1 1/2 cups corn starch — if you’re outside of the US it may be called corn flour 1/2 cup light-colored oil — such as vegetable oil, olive oil or baby oil Step Add play sand to a large bowl. Add cornstarch and whisk together to combine. Pour in the olive oil slowly, stir as you pour. When the mixture gets too difficult to mix with a spoon or spatula, finish kneading together by hand.

gac

Ingredients: 4 ounces school glue 1/2 cup water 1/4 cup hot water 1/2 teaspoon Borax food coloring
Mix the glue and 1 /2 cup of water together. Add the food coloring. Set aside. In a measuring glass, dissolve 1/2 teaspoon Borax with 1 / 4 cup of warm water. Stir until it’s completely dissolved. Add the borax water to the glue mixture and mix well, until the Gak forms a blob around your spoon.

Hand Soen Doll

Materials needed: Fabric (for body, clothing, and hair) Thread Needles Scissors Stuffing Embellishments (buttons, ribbons, etc.)
Instructions:
1. Cut out the pattern pieces for the doll’s body, clothing, and hair from the fabric.
2. Sew the body pieces together, leaving a small opening for stuffing.
3. Stuff the body with stuffing until it reaches your desired firmness.
4. Sew up the opening and shape the doll’s body.
5. Sew the clothing pieces together and dress the doll.
6. Sew on the hair pieces and embellishments to complete the doll.
